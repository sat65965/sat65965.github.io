Welcome

The University of Georgia Infant Research Laboratory, directed by Dr. Janet Frick, conducts studies on the development of visual attention and learning in young infants. Most of our studies involve parents bringing their babies (aged 3-12 months) to our Research Center, located on the first floor of the Psychology Building on the UGA campus, for a one-time visit. Click on the research link below to learn more about the specific studies we are doing right now, and to find out how you can participate. Parents are welcome to bring their older children to the testing session. We have a play area with books and toys, and have extra staff on hand to play with siblings. Our FREE parking area is right next to the building, and we will wait outside for you with a parking pass. We greatly appreciate your interest!

 

Don't forget to follow us on Twitter and like our Facebook page to keep up with current Infant Lab news!

Publication Alert!

The first batch of data from our infant visual processing speed (CFF) study has been published! This publication looks only at the data from babies in the breastfed group. A huge thank you to all of the babies who participated in this study (and their parents, of course). We really appreciate your contributions to our understanding of infant visual development!
